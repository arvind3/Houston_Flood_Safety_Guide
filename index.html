What Is Flood Risk?

Flooding is when water covers land that is usually dry. Flash floods are especially dangerous because they strike with little warning and cause massive damage.

Floods can destroy buildings and homes and carry harmful, polluted water that poses serious health risks.

Nature helps fight flooding: plants, trees, and shrubs reduce flood risk by limiting runoff water and increasing rain retention. Solutions like permeable pavements and rain gardens also make a big difference.

Use the FEMA Flood Map or National Risk Index to check your flood risk based on your location.

During a Flood

Follow this decision tree to stay safe when floodwaters rise.

Are you indoors or in a vehicle?
Indoors
Get to the highest open point (roof, not attic or basement). Call 9-1-1 if needed. Stay away from flood waters.
In a Vehicle
Is water rising inside the car?
Yes
Get on the roof of the car and call emergency services immediately.
No
Stay inside the vehicle. Do not attempt to drive through flood water.
⚠

Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters. 6 inches can knock you down; 12 inches can sweep away a vehicle.

🚫

Never cross bridges over fast-moving water or drive on underground or barricaded roads.

📱

Evacuate if ordered. Stay updated on alerts and notifications at all times.

📏

Water depth is easily underestimated. Never assume you can judge how deep it is.

Evacuation vs. Shelter-In-Place

🚶 Evacuation

  • Follow all local evacuation procedures
  • Use the FEMA app to find shelter locations
  • Confirm shelters allow pets before heading out
  • Keep your car fueled and your flood kit inside
  • If you don't have a car, contact local emergency offices
  • Turn off gas, electricity, and other home systems before leaving

🏠 Shelter-In-Place

  • Get to an open, high place (e.g., roof)
  • Avoid attics and basements — you could get trapped
  • Call 9-1-1 if you need rescue
  • Have your fire extinguisher and sealing material ready
  • If water surrounds your car, stay inside; if it enters, climb to the roof

After a Flood

Recovery takes time. Follow these steps in order to stay safe.

1

Wait for the All-Clear

Only return home when local authorities say it is safe. Follow all government orders.

2

Avoid Flood Water

Don't drink, touch, or go near flood water. It may contain sewage, chemicals, and dangerous debris.

3

Watch for Hazards

Be alert for diseases, chemicals, animals, downed power lines, and other dangerous objects.

4

Document Everything

Take photographs of all damage before cleaning up. Hire a flood adjuster for insurance claims, then file your claim.

5

Protect Yourself

Wear safe clothing, gloves, boots, and masks during cleanup. Disinfect items with a bleach solution.

6

Check for Mold

Do not enter buildings with visible or smellable mold. Mold can cause serious health problems.
